# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Modern Rwandan consumers, primarily mobile-first shoppers in Rwanda and East Africa. They value speed, trust, and a shopping experience that feels contemporary and local. They are comfortable with mobile money payments and expect fast delivery confirmation. Context: browsing on a phone, often on mobile data, in mixed lighting conditions.

## Product Purpose

Rwanda Market is an e-commerce storefront that lets Rwandan consumers browse, discover, and buy products with same-day or next-day delivery. Success means a shopper lands on the page, trusts the brand immediately, finds what they need, and completes a purchase. The landing page is the brand's handshake.

## Brand Personality

Confident, local, modern. Not a Western SaaS clone — a brand that looks like it was built for Rwanda, not adapted from a template. Warm without being soft. Fast without being cluttered.

## Anti-references

- Generic Western SaaS landing page: cream/sand background, hero metric stats, floating glassmorphism mock cards, green+purple gradient text. The current site is already partially here.
- Jumia/Aliexpress visual density: product-dump catalog aesthetic, competing banners, orange-price-sticker energy.
- Frictionless-tech-startup: blob animations, glow effects, "seamless experience" copy.

## Design Principles

1. **Local confidence first.** The brand should read as distinctly Rwandan, not as a Western startup that landed in Kigali. Typography, imagery, and copy choices carry this — not the color of a palette.
2. **Trust through clarity.** Rwandan shoppers are making real purchasing decisions. Product information, prices, and delivery promises must be unambiguous. Decoration that obscures information is a bug.
3. **Mobile is the primary canvas.** Design mobile layouts first, not last. Touch targets, readable prices, and one-thumb navigation are baseline requirements.
4. **Show, don't mock.** The current hero uses abstract floating card placeholders instead of real product imagery. Real products, real context, real delivery proof points beat wireframe stand-ins.
5. **Earn the accent color.** Green reads as Rwanda (the flag, the landscape, the brand of a country rebuilding). Use it with intention and commitment, not as a gradient gradient-text decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Mobile-first touch targets (44×44px minimum). French-language UI — avoid idioms that don't translate. Consider users on slower connections: image optimization and meaningful loading states required. Reduced motion support already present in globals.css — maintain it.
